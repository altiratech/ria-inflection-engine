## Three Buyer-Specific Value Propositions

### 1. Outsourced Compliance Providers

Primary value:
- identify which client firms or disclosure changes deserve review now without manually rereading every update

What they get:
- ranked review priority across many firms
- visible evidence behind each flag
- earlier detection of meaningful disclosure change
- better leverage for senior compliance reviewers

Good language:

`Cover more client firms with less manual triage while keeping your review reasoning evidence-backed and defensible.`

### 2. RIAs and In-House CCOs

Primary value:
- self-surveillance and exam readiness from the firm’s own disclosure changes

What they get:
- an outside-in view of their own filing changes
- faster identification of review burden created by recent updates
- self-benchmarking against similar firms
- a cleaner way to prepare for annual review and exam readiness work

Good language:

`See where your recent public disclosure changes may have increased review burden, and compare that posture to firms like yours.`

### 3. Benchmarking and Surveillance Programs

Primary value:
- peer-relative analytics and hidden-signal discovery across a market segment

What they get:
- peer benchmarking and self-benchmarking
- non-obvious pattern detection across filings and themes
- theme-aware review prioritization
- a better surveillance layer than static firm directories or spreadsheet watchlists

Good language:

`Find the firms, themes, and disclosure patterns that stand out from the cohort before they disappear into manual workflow.`

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Upstream, Not Downstream

Most compliance tooling starts after a review process has already begun.
This product starts earlier by detecting meaningful change in the public disclosures themselves.

### 2. Evidence Before Abstraction

The value is not a black-box label.
The value is a ranked, review-oriented view of change with visible evidence behind it.

### 3. Hidden Signals In Fragmented Data

The product is strongest where manual review is weakest:
- change over time
- cross-firm comparison
- pattern recognition across fragmented disclosures

### 4. More Leverage For Compliance Teams

The product helps experienced reviewers spend less time finding what changed and more time deciding what it means.

## Recommended One-Paragraph Description

`RIA Inflection Engine is a compliance-intelligence and supervisory-analytics product for RIAs and their compliance partners. It monitors public adviser disclosures over time, detects meaningful section-level filing changes, maps those changes to current SEC themes, and publishes evidence-backed review outputs with peer context. The result is a clearer way to identify hidden review signals, prioritize what deserves closer attention, and benchmark firms against similar peers without drifting into workflow software or black-box risk scoring.`
